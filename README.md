HR Workforce & Attrition Analysis

1️⃣ 📌 Problem Statement

               Employee attrition is one of the biggest challenges faced by organizations, leading to increased hiring costs, productivity loss, and instability in workforce planning.

This project aims to analyze HR employee data to:

    Understand workforce structure

    Identify reasons for employee attrition

    Detect high-risk employees

    Evaluate employee satisfaction and performance
    
    Support data-driven HR decision-making

2️⃣ 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset used is the IBM HR Attrition Dataset (100K records version).

   Key Columns:

        EmployeeID
        
        Age
        
        Gender
        
        Department
        
        JobRole
        
        MonthlyIncome
        
        TotalWorkingYears
        
        YearsAtCompany
        
        Attrition (Yes/No)
        
        OverTime
        
        JobSatisfaction
        
        PerformanceRating
        
        WorkLifeBalance
        
   Data Type:
      
      Structured tabular HR data

3️⃣ 📊 KPI Definitions

🟢 Total Employees

    Total number of employees in the organization.

🔴 Attrition Count

    Number of employees who left the organization.

📉 Attrition Rate
 
     AttritionRate=TotalEmployees/AttritionCount
   
	​
🟡 Retention Rate
 
      RetentionRate=1−AttritionRate

😊 Average Job Satisfaction

      Average score of employee satisfaction (scale 1–4)

⚖️ Average Work-Life Balance

     Average score representing employee balance between work and personal life

📈 Average Performance Rating

     Average employee performance score

5️⃣ 🔍 Key Findings
    
    Sales department has the highest attrition rate
    
    Employees working overtime are more likely to leave
    
    Junior employees show higher turnover compared to seniors
    
    Low salary group has higher attrition risk
    
    Job satisfaction strongly impacts employee retention
    
    Work-life balance is a key factor in employee performance

6️⃣ 💡 Recommendations

    Improve work-life balance policies
    
    Reduce excessive overtime workload
    
    Increase engagement programs for junior employees
    
    Provide salary restructuring for low-income groups
    
    Strengthen employee satisfaction monitoring system
    
    Introduce retention strategies for high-risk employees
